The Animal Rehoming Service

UX/UI Case Study

The Animal Rehoming Service (TARS) is a Not-for-profit started by Michaela Newell in 2000. TARS acts as a mediator between people looking to rehome their animal and those looking to adopt. In addition to this, TARS provides veternary care for animals who are homeless.

Ideation

User research

By conducting user interview and competitor Analysis, we found out that users would be less likely to adopt from an outdated website, or a website that they determined as being poorly designed and lack of information. Around 66% of users want the ability to apply for adoption online

Problem statement

The Animal Rehoming Service website is significantly outdated and in desperate need of a facelift. Are we able to assist TARS in their overarching goal of reducing animal homelessness, providing veternary care for animals who do not have access and funding their projects.

User definition

The typical user of the Animal Rehoming Service website is who wants to find a new companion to enrich her children's lives but needs accurate information to make the right decision. The users would like the website brand to be trustworthy and they usually get frustrated if the procedure of adoption/fostering is complicated.
